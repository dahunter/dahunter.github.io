I'm Dani

I'm a hard-working, sooth-seeking software developer in training. I'm interested in learning all that I can about web development from end to end. I'm intent on making development my full-time gig, and am currently on the road to making it happen. Take a look at my journey so far:

Lil Dani

Being the self-starter that I am, I began honing my skills for software development just after birth. As a small child, I was curious and inquisitive--always wanting to try new things and determined to do everything for myself. I asked questions constantly, which tended to get me into trouble. A toddler can only ask "why?" so many times before the answer becomes "because I said so!"

In elementary school I developed an interest in art and math. I enjoyed the creativity and problem solving involved, and those aspects became the focus of my hobbies outside of school. My interests later extended to science and then to philosophy, as I continued to ask questions and considered how things could be made better. This handful of characteristics--curiosity, independence, creativity and problem solving, is what motivates me today.

Radmin

Development first caught my attention when a departmental project at work got me collaborating with the engineering team. I had been hired on to manage customer invoicing via an internal proprietary billing application. As I worked together with the engineers on bug fixes and upkeep, I began to understand a little more about the work they were doing. The billing app proved to be clunky and inefficient, making long and tedious work out of something that could potentially be fully-automated. I worked directly with developers and product management to plan out a complete redesign of the app from end to end.

Radmin (Radiate Admin) was intended to be an all-in-one internal solution for managing our clients' accounts, but the project was shelved when business priorities shifted and development time was redirected elsewere. This got me thinking that if the engineers didn't have time, I could learn to build the billing parts I needed myself.

Why Programming

Merge

When deciding what I wanted to "do" when I grew up, I used to think I'd have to pick either creativity or logic. Right brain or left brain. Artist or astronaut. Turns out development is the best of both.

Bits to Polish

My need to understand why something is the way it is means i'm constantly seeking context. I'm interested in the big picture in relation to the small pieces, and strive to understand how each part of a project interacts with the other.

Problem Solving

There is nothing better than building something with your own two hands. And then when that something breaks or is lacking, finding a way to fix it.

Continual Learning

A lifetime of learning is what I'm looking for, and is exactly what this industry provides. The development community values education and in my experience is very welcoming to newcomers. Mentorship has been essential to my progress and I'd want to be a similar resource for others in the future.

Outro

blah blah blah blah not done yet. Since my first glimpse into the development industry, I knew it was something I wanted to learn more about. But the more I've dabbled the more I've become convinced that it's exactly what I'm built for. I'm looking forward to a lifetime of learning and creating within the industry. and in contributing to the evolution of the industry, mentoring. Front-end, back-end, game development, bits and bytes, emerging languages--I'd like to know it all. and in putting that knowledge to work both professionally and personally.
