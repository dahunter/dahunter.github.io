Lil Dani

Self-starter that I am, I began honing my skills for software development just after birth. As a small child, I was curious and inquisitive--always wanting to try new things and determined to do everything for myself. I asked questions constantly, which tended to get me into trouble. A toddler can only ask "why?" so many times before the answer becomes "because I said so!"

In elementary school I developed an interest in art and math. I enjoyed the creativity and problem solving involved, and those aspects became the focus of my hobbies outside of school. My interests later extended to science and then to philosophy, as I continued to ask questions and considered how things could be made better. This handful of characteristics--curiosity, independence, creativity and problem solving, is what motivates me today.

Why Programming

Merge

When deciding what I wanted to "do" when I grew up, I used to think I'd have to pick either creativity or logic. Right brain or left brain. Artist or astronaut. Turns out development is the best of both.

Bits to Polish

My need to understand why something is the way it is means i'm constantly seeking context. I'm interested in the big picture in relation to the small pieces, and strive to understand how each part of a project interacts with the other.

Problem Solving

Is it just me, or is there nothing better than building something with your own two hands? And then when that something breaks or is lacking, finding a way to fix it? I must have been a mechanic in another life.

Continual Learning

A lifetime of learning is what I'm looking for, and is exactly what this industry provides. The development community values education and in my experience is very welcoming to newcomers. Mentorship has been essential to my progress and I'd want to be a similar resource for others in the future.
