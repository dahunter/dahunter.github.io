Radmin

Development first caught my attention when a departmental project at work got me collaborating with the engineering team. I had been hired on to manage customer invoicing via an internal proprietary billing application. As I worked together with the engineers on bug fixes and upkeep, I began to understand a little more about the work they were doing. The billing app proved to be clunky and inefficient, making long and tedious work of something that could potentially be fully-automated. I worked directly with developers and product management to plan out a complete redesign of the app from end to end.

Radmin (Radiate Admin) was intended to be an all-in-one internal solution for managing our clients' accounts, but the project was shelved when business priorities shifted and development time was redirected elsewere. This got me thinking that if the engineers didn't have the time for it, I could learn to build the billing parts I needed myself.

Why Programming

Merge

When deciding what I wanted to "do" when I grew up, I used to think I'd have to pick either creativity or logic. Right brain or left brain. Artist or astronaut. Turns out development is the best of both.

Bits to Polish

My need to understand why something is the way it is means i'm constantly seeking context. I'm interested in the big picture as well as the small pieces, and strive to understand how each part of a project interacts with the other.

Problem Solving

There is nothing better than building something with your own two hands. And then when that something breaks or is lacking, finding a way to fix it.

Continual Learning

A lifetime of learning is what I'm looking for, and is exactly what this industry provides. The development community values education and in my experience is very welcoming to newcomers. Mentorship has been essential to my progress and I'd want to be a similar resource for others in the future.
